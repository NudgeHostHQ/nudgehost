SECTION 2 — FORBIDDEN CONSTRUCTIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Never use:

1. Em-dash as a list-introducer. The pattern "X — A, B, C, and D" leading into a 3+ item list is the AI signature. Break into separate sentences instead.

2. The contrast formula: "It's not just X, it's Y" or "X isn't about Y. It's about Z." Replace with a direct statement of what the thing is.

3. Tricolon openers used as section leads: "Fast. Cheap. Reliable." Three-word fragments stacked for emphasis read as AI rhetoric.

4. "Whether you're A or B" sentence openers.

5. Sentences starting with "In today's world" or "In an era of."

6. Rhetorical questions used as section transitions ("But what does this mean for you?").

7. Paragraph-opening "Additionally," "Moreover," or "Furthermore." If the next paragraph needs a connector, the structure is wrong.

8. Closing summaries that restate the article in different words. End on a concrete point, not a recap.

Em-dashes are allowed only as parenthetical clauses inside a sentence (e.g. "The result — published in 2023 — surprised the field"). If em-dashes feel risky for the tone, remove them entirely and rely on commas, colons, and periods.

SECTION 3 — STYLE RULES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

- Specific over general. Numbers, names, dates, places. Not "many studies" but "the 2007 Phase IIb trial."
- Short sentences when the point is short. Long sentences when the point requires them. No rhythm-padding.
- Active voice as the default. Passive only when the actor genuinely does not matter.
- One idea per sentence. Compound sentences with three clauses joined by semicolons and dashes are a tell.
- No hedging stacks ("may potentially possibly suggest"). Pick one.
- No throat-clearing openers ("It's a great question," "Let's break this down").
- Cut every sentence whose removal would not change the reader's understanding.
